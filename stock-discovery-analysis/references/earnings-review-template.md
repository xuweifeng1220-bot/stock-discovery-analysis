# Earnings Review Template

Use this template for quarterly/annual results, earnings calls, guidance updates, investor days, or post-results stock moves.

## Objective

Determine whether new results changed:

- Business quality
- Growth trajectory
- Margin structure
- Cash flow quality
- Management credibility
- Market expectation
- Valuation assumptions
- Thesis validity

## Step 1: Result Snapshot

| Metric | Actual | Consensus / Prior Expectation | Prior Period | Surprise | Thesis Impact |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---|

Classify each surprise:

- **Core**: changes long-term value.
- **Timing**: shifts quarters but not thesis.
- **Accounting**: non-cash or presentation effect.
- **Noise**: no material impact.

## Step 2: Guidance and Management Tone

Extract:

- Guidance change
- Management explanation
- Demand comments
- Pricing comments
- Cost/margin comments
- Inventory/channel comments
- Capex comments
- Competitive comments
- Capital allocation comments

Ask:

- Did management confirm or weaken the original thesis?
- Did they introduce a new bottleneck?
- Did they rely on vague language where numbers are needed?

## Step 3: Model Assumption Changes

| Assumption | Previous View | New Evidence | Revised View | Confidence |
|---|---|---|---|---|

Update only assumptions that evidence actually changes:

- Revenue growth
- Gross margin
- Operating leverage
- Capex intensity
- Working capital
- Unit economics
- Market share
- Segment mix
- Terminal multiple

## Step 4: Expectation Gap

State what the market likely expected before results and what changed after results.

| Market Assumption | Confirmed? | Broken? | Still Unclear? | Evidence |
|---|---|---|---|---|

Do not confuse a stock move with business truth. A strong report can still be bad if valuation required perfection; a weak report can still be interesting if pessimism was excessive.

## Step 5: Thesis Status

Classify the thesis:

- **Strengthened**: long-term driver improved.
- **Intact**: noise or timing issue only.
- **Weakened**: key assumption deteriorated.
- **Invalidated**: original business logic no longer holds.
- **Insufficient evidence**: more data needed.

## Step 6: Post-Earnings Action

Output:

- **What changed**
- **What did not change**
- **What the market may be mispricing**
- **Valuation assumption to revise**
- **Next evidence to monitor**
- **Whether the stock deserves upgrade, downgrade, or watchlist**

Do not output a buy/sell instruction. Output research support and decision conditions.
