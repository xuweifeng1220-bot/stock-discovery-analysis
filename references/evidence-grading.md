# Evidence Grading

Use this evidence scale for Candidate Intake and any stock analysis where facts, assumptions, and inference may be mixed.

## Evidence Levels

| Level | Evidence Type | Examples | Allowed Use |
|---|---|---|---|
| A | Primary official evidence | Company announcements, financial reports, regulatory filings, signed contracts, exchange documents, official disclosures | Can support core judgments |
| B | Strong operating evidence | Management guidance, backlog disclosure, customer filings, industry price data, explicit industry data, authoritative research, first-hand meeting notes | Can support core judgments if source/date are clear |
| C | Secondary evidence | News, sell-side opinions, hiring data, supply-chain reports, industry observation, credible unofficial channels | Auxiliary only; cannot carry the core thesis alone |
| D | Hypothesis or inference | AI inference, market rumors, logical deduction, unverified assumptions, user or AI subjective judgment | Hypothesis only; cannot support promotion into watchlist |

## Hard Rules

- A/B evidence may support core candidate advancement.
- C evidence may only support context, direction, or follow-up questions.
- D evidence must be labeled as a hypothesis.
- If a candidate mainly depends on C/D evidence, the conclusion must be **return to candidate pool** or **wait for user confirmation**, not **recommend enter watchlist**.
- If source/date are missing for a key fact, downgrade evidence confidence or mark **Data gap**.

## Labeling

Every important statement should be labeled as one of:

- **Fact**: public or source-backed information.
- **AI hypothesis**: inference made from available information.
- **User hypothesis**: user's industry view, private judgment, or supplied assumption.
- **Unknown**: important item not yet verified.
