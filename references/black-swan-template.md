# Black-Swan Analysis Template

Use this template when the user asks for black-swan thinking, anti-consensus analysis, hidden upside/downside, asymmetric payoff, or when the stock price depends heavily on consensus expectations.

## Core Question

Start with one sentence:

- **What consensus expectation is the market currently pricing?**

Then test whether the market may be wrong on:

- Demand
- Supply
- Price
- Margin
- Competition
- Regulation
- Financing
- Technology path
- Customer adoption
- Capital market liquidity

## Positive Black Swan

| Event | Broken Consensus | Trigger Signal | Business Impact Path | Valuation Impact | Probability | Payoff | Evidence to Watch |
|---|---|---|---|---|---:|---:|---|

Use positive black swans for:

- Unexpected policy support
- Supply shock benefiting the company
- Demand acceleration
- Competitor failure
- New customer/category validation
- Financing cost improvement
- Technology route becoming dominant
- Industry capacity discipline

## Negative Black Swan

| Event | Broken Bull Assumption | Trigger Signal | Business Damage Path | Valuation Damage | Probability | Drawdown Risk | Defense |
|---|---|---|---|---|---:|---:|---|

Use negative black swans for:

- Valuation multiple collapse
- Demand destruction
- Policy reversal
- Subsidy or tariff shock
- Financing stress
- Margin squeeze
- Inventory glut
- Customer concentration failure
- Technology route failure
- Capital exit from crowded trades

## Consensus Mispricing Map

| Market Belief | Why It May Be Wrong | Who Benefits If Wrong | Who Gets Hurt If Wrong | Verification Signal |
|---|---|---|---|---|

Force the analysis to identify the **real beneficiary** in the value chain. A good sector thesis can still produce a bad stock if value accrues upstream, downstream, or to customers instead of the listed company.

## Risk-Reward Asymmetry

| Case | Downside Cushion | Upside Path | Time Horizon | Key Condition | Position Implication |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|

Classify the opportunity:

- **Downside-protected upside option**: acceptable for deeper research.
- **High-upside but no cushion**: speculative; require strict sizing.
- **Crowded consensus with limited upside**: avoid or wait.
- **Good story but bad price**: watchlist only.

## Invalidation Triggers

List concrete conditions that kill the thesis:

- Revenue/margin evidence fails
- Capacity cycle turns against the company
- Policy support weakens
- Competitor response compresses returns
- Valuation already prices the optimistic case
- Technical breakdown signals crowded exit
- Liquidity regime changes

## Required Output

End every black-swan analysis with:

- **Best positive black swan**
- **Most dangerous negative black swan**
- **Most likely consensus error**
- **What is already priced in**
- **What evidence changes the view**
- **Whether the current risk-reward is worth deeper research**
