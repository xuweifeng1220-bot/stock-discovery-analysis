# Market Research Workflow

Use this workflow when the user starts from a theme, industry, policy direction, technology trend, commodity move, or macro question and wants to discover investable stocks.

## Goal

Transform a theme into a researchable stock pool:

**Theme -> industry structure -> value-chain map -> real beneficiaries -> peer group -> candidate shortlist -> research priorities**

## Step 1: Define the Theme

State the theme in one sentence and classify it:

- Demand expansion
- Supply constraint
- Cost decline
- Policy push
- Technology transition
- Domestic substitution
- Capital cycle reversal
- Liquidity or sentiment theme

Then state whether the theme is:

- **Long-cycle structural**: can support multi-year compounding.
- **Mid-cycle industry rotation**: depends on supply-demand timing.
- **Short-term catalyst**: useful for timing but weak as thesis.
- **Pure narrative**: requires skepticism and valuation discipline.

## Step 2: Map the Value Chain

Build a value-chain table.

| Segment | Role | Pricing Power | Bottleneck | Listed Companies | Real Beneficiary? |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|

Force the distinction between:

- Companies that **tell the story**
- Companies that **capture profit**
- Companies that **enable the chain but lack pricing power**
- Companies whose exposure is too small to matter

## Step 3: Identify the Profit Pool

For each segment, estimate:

- Market size
- Value added per unit
- Margin structure
- Capacity constraint
- Customer concentration
- Substitution risk
- Competitive intensity

The best stock is often not the most visible company, but the company with the best combination of **value capture, scarcity, and valuation safety**.

## Step 4: Build Peer Group and Comps

Create a comparable universe:

| Company | Ticker | Market | Segment | Revenue Exposure | Growth | Margin | Valuation | Liquidity | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---|

Use comps to ask:

- Which company has the cleanest exposure?
- Which company has the strongest earnings sensitivity?
- Which company already reflects the story?
- Which company is ignored because it is boring, small, or misunderstood?

## Step 5: Shortlist Candidates

Rank candidates by:

1. **Business exposure**
2. **Value-chain position**
3. **Expectation gap**
4. **Valuation safety**
5. **Catalyst visibility**
6. **Downside protection**
7. **Data availability**

| Rank | Company | Why It May Work | What Is Priced In | Main Missing Evidence | Research Priority |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|

## Step 6: Reject Weak Ideas

Explicitly reject candidates that fail because:

- Theme exposure is immaterial.
- Valuation already prices the optimistic case.
- Company is a commodity taker without pricing power.
- Business model is unclear.
- Catalyst is noisy but not economically material.
- The real beneficiary is another part of the value chain.

## Required Output

End with:

- **Best candidate**
- **Best value-chain segment**
- **Most overhyped segment**
- **Most important data gap**
- **Next 3 research actions**
